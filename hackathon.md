## Award Structure

Prizes for Sui Overflow 2026 will follow a **split distribution model** designed to encourage continued development beyond the hackathon:

- **50% of the prize** will be awarded upon announcement of winners
- **50% of the prize** will be awarded after successful mainnet deployment

If a winning team has already deployed their project to mainnet by the time winners are announced in August, they will receive **100% of the prize upfront**.

## Notes

- Mainnet deployment must meet the minimum functional requirements as defined by the Sui team and/or track sponsors.
- Teams are encouraged to continue building post-hackathon to unlock the full prize amount.
- Additional support, opportunities, and ecosystem pathways may be available for top-performing teams.

# Demo Day Requirements

Projects that are shortlisted will advance to Demo Day and must meet the following requirements:

### Attendance

At least one team member must be present during your assigned Demo Day session.

### Demo Day Schedule

**July 20, 2026**

- Agentic Web Track
- Walrus Track

**July 21, 2026**

- DeFi & Payments Track
- DeepBook Track

\***_Detailed schedule will be shared once the shortlisted teams have been announced._**

### Presentation Requirements

Shortlisted teams will be required to:

- Present their project live to judges
- Demonstrate a working version of their project
- Answer questions from judges during the Q&A portion

### Demo Day Format

- **Presentation Time:** 5 minutes per team
- **Q&A:** Up to 2 additional minutes, if time allows
- Timing is strictly enforced

Please ensure your presentation is concise and focused on:

- The problem you are solving
- Your solution
- Technical implementation
- Why you built on Sui
- Future roadmap

### Absence Policy

If a team is absent during their assigned presentation slot, they will be called again at the end of the session.

Failure to appear after a second call may result in forfeiture from the competition.

### Meeting Link & Calendar Invite

- Zoom meeting links will be shared directly via email.
- A calendar invitation will also be sent to shortlisted teams.
- All participants are encouraged to join a few minutes before their scheduled presentation time.

### Livestream

Demo Day sessions will also be streamed live on YouTube for the broader community.

## FAQ

- **What are the eligibility requirements to participate in Overflow?**
  - The project must be deployed to Sui mainnet or testnet by the time of shortlisting and demo day.
  - The submission must either:
    - be a completely new project built between **May 7th to June 21st, 2026**, or
    - introduce substantial new functionality, features, or integrations to an existing Sui project developed during the same period.
  - The project team must be available to participate in the virtual Demo Day session.
  - At least one member of the project team must pass KYC in order to receive prizes.
  - Participants residing in OFAC-sanctioned regions are not eligible to participate and win.
- **How do I become eligible for the University Award?**
  To be eligible for the University Awards, please head to the **My Profile** section on your DeepSurge account and make sure to update your profile details to indicate that you are currently a university student.
  You can update this under the “Are you currently a university student?” field in your profile settings.
  !Screenshot 2026-06-08 at 6.37.22 PM copy.png
- **As a student, will my project only be considered for the University Award?**
  No. Student teams are eligible for both the main track prizes and the University Award. The University Award is an additional recognition category and does not replace eligibility for the main 4 tracks.
- **Is Sui Overflow 2026 fully online?**
  Yes. Sui Overflow 2026 is conducted entirely online, including project submissions, judging, and Demo Day presentations.
- **Is Demo Day in-person?**
  No, Demo Day for Sui Overflow 2026 will be conducted virtually. Shortlisted teams will present their projects remotely to judges and ecosystem partners.
- **Do I need to speak English during Demo Day?**
  Speaking in English is highly encouraged. If you are not comfortable presenting live in English, you may prepare a video presentation with English narration or subtitles. The goal is to ensure judges can understand your project and its functionality.
- **What if my project fits into multiple tracks?**
  - Projects may naturally overlap across multiple tracks. However, each submission may only be submitted under one primary track. Teams should choose the track that best represents the core focus and value proposition of their project.
- **Within the tracks, there are subtracks. Are subtracks competing separately?**
  No, subtracks are intended only as guides or inspiration to help teams decide what type of project to build within a broader track theme. Projects will still compete within the main track itself, and winners will be selected across the overall track regardless of subtrack category.
- **What is the "Core Track" checkbox? Do I need to tick it?**
  The Core Track checkbox applies to submissions under the Agentic Web and DeFi & Payments tracks. If you are submitting under Walrus or DeepBook, you do not need to select it. There are no separate bounty categories associated with this checkbox.
- **How do I find teammates on DeepSurge?**
  If you do not have a team yet, you can use the "Find a Team" section on DeepSurge to connect with other participants who are looking for teammates.
- **Can I be a part of multiple teams?**
  Yes, participants may contribute to multiple projects or teams, provided the projects are meaningfully different and are not simply replicas or slight variations of each other.
- **What is the minimum team size?**
  Teams must have at least 2 members. There is no requirement to have more than the minimum as long as all team members are properly listed in the submission.
- **Can I reuse part of my code from my previous work?**
  Yes, if you have the legal rights to the code, you may reuse components such as frontend frameworks, UI libraries, boilerplate infrastructure, or tooling. However, projects should still demonstrate substantial new development completed during the hackathon period. If significant portions of the project were pre-existing, teams should clearly disclose this in their submission.
- **Do I need to open-source my project repo?**
  Projects should be accessible for review during judging. If you prefer to keep your repository private during development, we recommend making it accessible during the shortlisting and judging period so judges, sponsors, and reviewers can properly evaluate your work.
- **Are AI-assisted tools allowed?**
  Yes, AI-assisted development tools are allowed and expected. Projects will not be judged based on the amount of manually written code, but rather on product quality, technical execution, creativity, usability, and real-world value.
- **Can I continue updating my project after submission?**
  Yes. You may continue updating your repo after submitting your project. However, any changes, features, or improvements added after the submission deadline will not be considered during judging.
- **Can I get help from mentors during the hackathon?**
  Yes. Participants are encouraged to book office hours with mentors and technical experts throughout the hackathon. Mentor support is available to all participants.
- **How are projects evaluated?**
  Projects will primarily be evaluated based on:
  - Product & UX
  - Real-world application
  - Technical implementation
  - Presentation & vision
  Judges may also consider meaningful usage of Sui-specific capabilities and long-term ecosystem potential depending on the track.
- **Will I need to complete KYC to participate?**
  KYC is generally only required if your project wins a prize or award. At least one member of the winning team must successfully complete KYC in order to receive the prize.
- **If my project wins, when will I receive the prize?**
  Winning teams receive 50% of the total prize amount after results are announced. The remaining portion of the prize is released once the project has been successfully deployed to Sui Mainnet and meets the post-award requirements.
